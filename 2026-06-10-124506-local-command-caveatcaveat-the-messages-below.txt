❯ is this the same as the supervisor?                                           

● No — they're completely different things. It's worth being clear on this
  because it trips people up:

  The service account is not a person at all. It's a robot identity that Google
  Cloud creates so your code can log into the spreadsheet on its own, without a
  human signing in. It has an email-looking address
  (standup-bot@…iam.gserviceaccount.com), but no one ever reads mail there or
  logs into it — it's just a key the scripts use.

  A supervisor, by contrast, is a human being. In this project there's no
  "supervisor" role anywhere in the code — the only people it knows about are
  the interns listed in the config tab (by their Slack User IDs).

  So the cast of identities here is:

  ┌──────────────────┬─────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┐
  │     Identity     │     What it is      │        Where it appears        │

❯ Yes, the supervisor won't be me. Im just creating this app for the supervisor 
  and interns to use.                                                           

● Got it — so you're the builder, and the supervisor + interns are the end 
  users. That changes two things worth deciding now, before you create accounts.

  1. Whose accounts should own the infrastructure?

  Right now the setup guide assumes one person owns everything. Since you're
  handing this off, you have a choice for each of the three platforms (Google,
  Slack, GitHub):

  - Build it under the supervisor's accounts — you do the setup while logged
  into their Google/Slack/GitHub, or with them present. Cleaner long-term:
  nothing breaks if you leave, and they control the data. The spreadsheet lives
  in their Drive, the bot in their Slack, the repo in their GitHub.
  - Build it under your accounts, then transfer — easier for you to do solo now,
  but you'll later need to transfer the GitHub repo, re-share the Sheet, and
  possibly reinstall the Slack app. More handoff steps, and the bot keeps
  depending on your accounts until you do.

  The thing to avoid: building it half under your accounts and forgetting to
  hand over the keys — then it silently breaks when you stop maintaining it.

  2. What does the supervisor actually do with the running app?
